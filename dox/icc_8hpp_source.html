21 /** \file
22  *
23  * ICC is a method that allows to take into account the influence
24  * of arbitrarily shaped dielectric interfaces. The dielectric
25  * properties of a dielectric medium in the bulk of the simulation
26  * box are taken into account by reproducing the jump in the electric
27  * field at the interface with charge surface segments. The charge
28  * density of the surface segments have to be determined
29  * self-consistently using an iterative scheme. It can at present
30  * be used with P3M, ELCP3M and MMM1D. For details see: @cite tyagi10a
31  *
32  * To set up ICC, first the dielectric boundary has to be modeled
33  * by ESPResSo particles n_0...n_0+n where n_0 and n have to be passed
34  * as a parameter to ICC.
35  *
36  * For the determination of the induced charges only the forces
37  * acting on the induced charges has to be determined. As P3M and the
38  * other Coulomb solvers calculate all mutual forces, the force
39  * calculation was modified to avoid the calculation of the short
40  * range part of the source-source force calculation. For different
41  * particle data organisation schemes this is performed differently.
42  */
